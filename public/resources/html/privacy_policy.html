D. Use by Us of IP Addresses, Cookies, Web Beacons, Single-pixel Gifs, and Information Saved by Other Technologies

LN and Third-Party Processors acting on our behalf use various Internet technologies which help us to manage the operations of our Services and track your usage behavior so that we can tailor information and advertisements to make your visits more enjoyable and meaningful. These Internet technologies include:

  • IP Addresses: An IP address is a number that is automatically assigned to a computer by the Internet service provider whenever the computer is connected to the Internet. Web servers, the computers that deliver web pages, automatically identify a computer by its IP address, and when a page is requested from a website, its servers log the IP address of the computer requesting the page.
  • Cookies: Cookies are text files containing small amounts of information which are downloaded to your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are then sent back to the originating website on each subsequent visit, or to another website that recognizes that cookie. Cookies are useful because they allow a website to recognize a user's device. You can find more information about cookies at: www.allaboutcookies.org.
  • Beacons and Single-pixel Gifs: Beacons are electronic tags. Certain pages on our Local Now Site contain web beacons (also known as Internet tags, pixel tags and clear Gifs). A pixel tag is an electronic image, often a single pixel (1x1), which is ordinarily not visible to users and may be associated with cookies on users' hard drives. These pixel tags collect anonymous data. These web beacons allow third parties to obtain information such as the IP address of the computer that downloaded the page on which the beacon appears, the URL of the page on which the beacon appears, the time the page containing the beacon was viewed, the type of browser used to view the page, and the information in cookies sent by the third party.
  • Local Shared Objects: Local Shared Objects are storage within your browser on your electronic device. In addition to the above cookies, we use Local Shared Objects such as HTML5 LocalStorage on our Local Now Site. These are used to enhance your user experience, for example, by storing your user preferences and settings, such as your volume/mute settings, and in connection with animated content on our Local Now Site. Local Shared Objects are similar to browser cookies but can store data more complex than simple text. By themselves, they cannot do anything to or with the data on your computer. Like other cookies, they can only access PII that you have provided on the Local Now Site and cannot be accessed by other websites. For more information and to find out how to disable them, you can consult the information provided at http://kb2.adobe.com/cps/526/52697ee8.html.

Your browser, such as Internet Explorer, Chrome, Firefox and Safari, may allow you to manage your cookies and Local Shared Objects such as HTML5 LocalStorage. Most browsers are initially set up to accept cookies. Each browser is a little different, so look at your browser's help feature that will tell you how to stop accepting new cookies, how to receive notification of new cookies, and how to disable existing cookies, as well as your options regarding other technologies identified in this section. Please note, however, that without cookies and the other technologies in this section you may not be able to take full advantage of all our Local Now Site features and some parts of the Local Now Site may not function properly. For more information about cookies and how to disable them, you can consult the information at www.allaboutcookies.org/manage-cookies/. Disabling cookies is device specific and may not work on all devices. At this time, we do not respond to web browser "do not track" signals.

LN contracts with certain Third-Party Processors to track, analyze and report data about the usage of our Services using these Internet technologies. These Third-Party Processors include Adobe and its Adobe Analytics product. To obtain information about the privacy and security practices of Adobe and your options to set your Adobe preferences, use the following link: http://www.adobe.com/privacy.html.

7. Rights for California Residents Under the California Consumer Privacy Act

In 2018 California passed the California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA").

Under the CCPA California residents may be afforded certain rights regarding a business' use of a consumer's personal information. These rights include:

  1. The right to request a business disclose what categories and specific pieces of personal information have been collected by the business as well as the sources of that information;
  2. The right to opt out of the sale of personal information to a third party;
  3. The right to request deletion of personal information collected from the consumer;
  4. The right to know the categories of third parties with whom personal information is shared;
  5. The right to know the categories of information that are sold or disclosed for a business purpose to third parties;
  6. The right to know the business or commercial purpose for collecting or selling personal information;
  7. The right to reach out to a supervisory authority; and
  8. The right to not be discriminated against for exercising any rights under the CCPA.
